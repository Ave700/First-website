Let's Get Yeasty!

What the hick'ity'heck is sourdough?

Sourdough is a catch all phrase for really bitter bread. Kind of, more precisley it is a type of bread that is conventionally naturally risen. Meaning there is no commercial yeasts involved in the levining process. I'll get into what exactly natural yeasts are later, but for now just know the commonly associated sour taste is due to them.

More on yeast

Now my artistic abilities really shine through in this diagram. So allow me to further explain the process that yeast do. Firstly the baker will mix flour and water, not equal parts but very close to. Then they will let the mixture sit open to the air. Natural yeasts floating about near the start will be attracted to the sugars inside the mixture. This sugars are actually existant in the flour already. Once the yeast take up home in the mixture they will begin to consume, fart, deficate, and breed. They release CO2 and Ethyl Alcohol during this process and the latter is what produces the iconic sour flavor. An interesting side effect of this process is that since the specific yeast in each area are unique, sourdough breads will have different flavors depending on where the bakery is. This is how San Franciso sourdough is set apart from the rest.
